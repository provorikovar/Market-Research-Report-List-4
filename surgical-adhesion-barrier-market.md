<p><p>Surgical adhesion barriers are used to prevent the formation of adhesions after surgery. Film formulations are thin sheets placed over tissues to create a physical barrier. Gel formulations are thick, viscous substances applied to tissues to prevent adhesion formation. Liquid formulations are solutions that are sprayed or applied as a coating to prevent adhesions. Each type of formulation offers unique benefits depending on the surgical procedure and location within the body, providing surgeons with options to effectively reduce the risk of postoperative adhesions.</p></p>
