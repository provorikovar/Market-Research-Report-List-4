<p><ul><li>Dynamic Orthotics</li><li>Static Orthotics</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Dynamic Orthotics are devices that provide support and help to improve function by allowing controlled movement, typically using springs or hinges to assist in walking or supporting the hip joint during motion. Static Orthotics, on the other hand, are designed to limit or restrict movement, providing stability and alignment to the hip joint. Both types of orthoses are used to manage conditions such as hip dysplasia, arthritis, or injuries, with dynamic orthotics allowing for some degree of movement and static orthotics providing rigid support.</p></p>
