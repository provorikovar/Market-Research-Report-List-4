<p><p>An Eddy Current Flaw Detector is a non-destructive testing device that uses electromagnetic induction to detect flaws or defects in conducting materials. It works by inducing an alternating current in a coil that creates a changing magnetic field, which in turn generates eddy currents in the material being tested. Changes in the material's electromagnetic properties, such as conductivity or permeability, can indicate the presence of defects that disrupt the flow of eddy currents.</p><p>The Eddy Current Flaw Detector Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.5% during the forecast period.
