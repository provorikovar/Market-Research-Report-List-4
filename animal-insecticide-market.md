<p><p>Animal insecticides can be classified into three main types based on their mode of action: Endoparasiticides, Ectoparasiticides, and Endectocides. Endoparasiticides are used to control parasites that live inside the animal's body, such as worms. Ectoparasiticides target parasites that live on the animal's skin or fur, like fleas and ticks. Endectocides have a dual action, targeting both internal and external parasites. Each type of insecticide plays a crucial role in protecting animals from harmful parasites and diseases.</p></p>
