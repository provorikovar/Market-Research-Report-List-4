<p><p>Atmospheric plasma systems are categorized into two main types based on pressure: low pressure and high pressure. Low-pressure plasma systems operate at pressures close to vacuum levels, typically below 1 atmosphere, while high-pressure systems operate at pressures closer to atmospheric levels. Low-pressure systems are commonly used for precision surface treatments and coating applications, while high-pressure systems are preferred for more aggressive cleaning and activation processes. Both types offer unique benefits and are chosen based on specific application requirements and desired outcomes.</p></p>
