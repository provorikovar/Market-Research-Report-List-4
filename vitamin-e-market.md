<p><p>Vitamin E is available in two main market types: Natural Vitamin E and Synthetic Vitamin E. Natural Vitamin E is derived from natural sources such as vegetable oils and nuts, and typically contains a mix of tocopherols and tocotrienols. Synthetic Vitamin E, on the other hand, is produced in a laboratory setting and typically only contains alpha-tocopherol. Both types of Vitamin E are used in various industries including food, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics, with natural Vitamin E often being preferred for its perceived health benefits and potentially higher bioavailability.</p></p>
