Solution Phase Synthesis (SPS) involves the step-by-step assembly of peptide chains in a liquid medium, while Solid Phase Peptide Synthesis (SPPS) involves the attachment of amino acids to a solid support, allowing for more efficient purification and isolation of the peptide. Both methods are widely used in the production of synthetic peptide vaccines for various diseases and have their own advantages and limitations.</p></p>
