<p><p>Electroencephalography (EEG) biosensors are classified into four types: dry EEG, semi-dry EEG, wet EEG, and others. Dry EEG sensors do not require conductive gel, making them more comfortable for long-term use. Semi-dry EEG sensors use a minimal amount of gel, striking a balance between comfort and signal quality. Wet EEG sensors use a significant amount of gel to improve signal quality but can be messy. Other types of EEG biosensors may involve different technologies or materials for data collection.</p></p>
