<p><p>Automated Optical Inspection (AOI) is a technology used to inspect printed circuit boards (PCBs), electronics assemblies, and semiconductor devices for defects such as missing components, incorrect components, short or open circuits, and soldering defects. AOI systems use cameras and specialized software to analyze images of the PCB to detect any anomalies. This technology helps to improve the quality and reliability of electronic products, reduce manufacturing costs, and increase production efficiency.</p><p>The Automated Optical Inspection (AOI) Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 14.4% during the forecast period. The increasing demand for high-quality electronic products, stringent quality standards, and the need for reducing manufacturing errors are driving the growth of the AOI market. Additionally, the adoption of Industry 4.0 and the increasing trend towards automation in manufacturing processes are contributing to the market expansion.</p><p>Latest trends in the AOI market include the integration of artificial intelligence and machine learning algorithms in AOI systems to improve defect detection accuracy and reduce false alarms. Additionally, the development of 3D AOI systems for inspecting complex PCB assemblies and advancements in imaging technology such as higher resolution cameras and advanced lighting techniques are further enhancing the capabilities of AOI systems. These trends are expected to continue driving the growth of the AOI market in the coming years.</p></p>

<p><p>Automated Optical Inspection (AOI) systems are used in the manufacturing process to detect defects in products. Two main types of AOI systems are Inline and Offline. </p><p>Inline AOI systems are integrated directly into the production line, allowing for real-time inspection as products move through the assembly process. Offline AOI systems, on the other hand, are separate from the production line and are typically used for more detailed and comprehensive inspections on a sampling of finished products. Both types play a crucial role in ensuring product quality and reducing defects in manufacturing processes.</p></p>
