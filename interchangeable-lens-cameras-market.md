<p><p>Interchangeable Lens Cameras are divided into two main market types based on the image sensor technology used: CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) and CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor). CCD sensors are known for their high-quality image output and low noise levels, making them ideal for professional photographers. On the other hand, CMOS sensors are more commonly found in consumer-level cameras due to their lower cost and power efficiency. Both technologies have their own sets of advantages and cater to different market segments within the interchangeable lens camera industry.</p></p>
