<p><p>Pacemakers are electronic devices implanted in the chest to help control abnormal heart rhythms by sending electrical impulses to the heart muscle to maintain a regular heartbeat. Implantable Cardioverter Defibrillators (ICDs) are devices that continuously monitor the heart rhythm and deliver an electrical shock to restore normal heart rhythm if a life-threatening arrhythmia is detected. Both Pacemakers and ICDs are crucial in managing heart conditions and improving the quality of life for patients with heart rhythm disorders.</p></p>
