<p><p>Oral thin films are a rapidly growing segment in the pharmaceutical market, with two main types: sublingual films and fast-dissolving dental/buccal films. Sublingual films are placed under the tongue for quick absorption into the bloodstream, bypassing the digestive system. Fast-dissolving dental/buccal films are applied to the inside of the cheek or on the gums, where they dissolve rapidly for immediate delivery of medication. Both types offer advantages such as convenience, ease of administration, and potentially faster onset of action compared to traditional oral dosage forms.</p></p>
