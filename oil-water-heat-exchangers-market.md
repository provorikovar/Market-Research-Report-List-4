<p><p>Oil-Water Heat Exchangers can be divided into two main types based on their power source: AC and DC. AC heat exchangers utilize alternating current electricity, which is commonly found in residential and commercial buildings. On the other hand, DC heat exchangers run on direct current power, often used in off-grid or remote locations where access to AC electricity may be limited. Both types of heat exchangers are designed to efficiently transfer heat between oil and water, providing effective temperature control in various applications.</p></p>
