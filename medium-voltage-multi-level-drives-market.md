<p><p>Medium Voltage Multi-level Drives are classified into two types based on cooling methods: Air Cooling and Water Cooling. Air Cooling drives use fans to dissipate heat generated during operation, making them suitable for applications where water availability is limited. On the other hand, Water Cooling drives use water as a coolant to maintain optimal operating temperature. These drives are preferred in applications where continuous operation at high power levels is required, as water has a higher heat capacity compared to air. Both types offer efficient cooling solutions for Medium Voltage Multi-level Drives.</p></p>
